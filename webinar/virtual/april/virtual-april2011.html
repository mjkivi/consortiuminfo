Attend:		 Board members, committee members, directors of standards of associations, consortia or other non-profit organizations

For any standards development organization, the technical process is the heart of the association. As a result, how that process is created and managed will be key to ensuring an entity’s ultimate success. Yet, ironically, the people responsible for setting up a new standards organization often don't know how to do it, or even where to begin. Even if they do, they will inevitably encounter numerous unforeseen obstacles that can bog down their technical mission, ranging from disputes involving intellectual property rights to conflicting member agendas.  

This webinar will outline proven best practices – covering both process and legal concerns – that have helped some of the largest industry associations work at peak efficiency and productivity. 

Join our experts ‒ Andy Freed, Mary McRae and Andy Updegrove ‒ on Friday, April 15, from Noon-1:00 p.m. (Eastern), as they cover:

  • Process Rules – Why You Need Them 
  • IPR Policies ‒ Why You Can't Live Without One 
  • Projects ‒ Requirements, Plans and Issues Tracking
  • The Chair ‒ Leader, Negotiator and Task Master

  As Vice President, Technical Committee Support, at Virtual, Inc., Mary McRae has 20 years of standards development experience, including:  development of rules of procedure, process and IPR policies, management of technical committees and implementation of tools and technologies.
